Robyn Rihanna Fenty

The fiercest woman in the music industry

Synopsis

Born Robyn Rihanna Fenty, on February 20, 1988, in Barbados, Rihanna signed with Def Jam records at age 16 and in 2005 released her first album Music of the Sun, which sold more than two million copies worldwide. She went on to release more albums and an array of hit songs, including "Unfaithful," "Umbrella," "Disturbia," "Take a Bow," "Diamonds" and "We Found Love." A global pop star with an unrelentingly edgy image, Rihanna has also won multiple industry accolades, including Grammys and MTV awards.

Timeline

  • 1988 - Singer Robyn Rihanna Fenty was born on February 20, 1988, in St. Michael Parish on the Caribbean island of Barbados.
  • 2003 - As a teenager, Rihanna formed a girl group with two classmates; when they were 15 years old, they scored an audition with music producer Evan Rodgers. Rihanna was picked over the other girls to become a solo artist.
  • 2004 - Rihanna left Barbados to move in with Rogers and his wife in Connecticut and work on recording a demo album.
  • 2005 - Rihanna lands an audition for Def Jam Records and its president, rapper Jay-Z. The hip-hop icon was wowed by Rihanna's voice and presence and he signed her on the spot.
  • August 2005 - Rihanna's first single, "Pon de Replay," reached No. 2 on the Billboard singles chart. Her first album, Music of the Sun, reached No. 10 on the Billboard albums chart.
  • 2006 - Rihanna released her second album, A Girl Like Me, spawning two major hits in "Unfaithful" and "SOS," Rihanna's first No. 1 single.
  • 2007 - Rihanna effected a transformation from cute teen pop princess to superstar and sex symbol with her third album, Good Girl Gone Bad, fueled by its smash hit lead single "Umbrella," featuring Jay-Z.
  • 2008 - "Umbrella" topped the Billboard singles chart and earned Rihanna her first Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration. The album reached No. 2 on the charts and also featured the singles "Shut Up and Drive" and "Don't Stop the Music," with the latter featuring a sample of Michael Jackson's "Wanna Be Startin' Somethin'." Good Girl Gone Bad: Reloaded, released the following year, scored further hits in "Disturbia," "Take a Bow" and "Hate That I Love You."
  • 2009 - Rihanna released Rated R in 2009 with the singles "Hard" and "Rude Boy."
  • 2010 - Her next album, Loud, was once again a commercial success behind the songs "What's My Name," "Only Girl (In the World)" and "S&M."
  • Besides her own laundry list of hit songs, Rihanna is also featured on a host of popular songs by other artists, including Jay-Z's "Run this Town," Eminem's "Love the Way You Lie," Maroon 5's "If I Never See Your Face Again" and Kanye West's "All of the Lights."
  • 2011 - Rihanna released her sixth studio album: Talk That Talk. The album included "We Found Love," a track with DJ Calvin Harris that won the 2013 Grammy Award for best short form music video.
  • 2012 - On her Grammy-winning album Unapologetic, Rihanna turned out such hits as the No. 1 Sia Furler tune "Diamonds" and "Stay," featuring Mikky Ekko. Rihanna also began to do movie work, co-starring in the sci-fi flick Battleship.
  • 2013 - She also worked with Coldplay on the track "Princess of China" and reached No. 1 again with another Eminem collaboration, "The Monster."
  • 2014 - The singer made a bold professional move as well: She switched from the Def Jam label to Jay-Z's Roc Nation.
  • 2015 - She later lent her voice to the lead character in the animated blockbuster Home. In August that year, NBC announced that Rihanna would be the key adviser on The Voice's ninth season.
  • Rihanna contributed vocals to the single "FourFiveSeconds," a collaboration with West and famed Beatle Paul McCartney, as well as releasing "B**** Better Have My Money," a tune allegedly inspired by court wranglings with her former manager that also featured a highly controversial, violent music video. That year, Rihanna also became the first artist in history to have 100 million singles digitally downloaded and streamed.
  • 2016 - In late January, Rihanna released her next album ANTi, allowing Jay-Z's online streaming site Tidal to exclusively feature the collection of tracks for a week. The lead single off the album is "Work," featuring rapper Drake.
